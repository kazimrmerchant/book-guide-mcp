# Sample Handbook: Talking to Users

## When to talk

Talk to users when you have a specific decision to inform, not when you want validation for a pitch.

## The rule of past behavior

Ask about the last time they did something, not what they would do in a hypothetical future. Hypotheses flatter; history constrains.

## Avoid leading questions

Bad: "Would a faster export feature help you?"
Better: "Walk me through the last time you exported data. What broke?"

## Five users, one pattern

Look for the same pain in multiple independent stories before you build. One vivid anecdote is a story; three aligned histories are a signal.

## Closing the loop

After shipping, return to the same users and ask what changed in their last real workflow — not whether they "like" the release.
